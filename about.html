A little about me

I was born and raised in New York City, and I am a proud alum of the NYC public school system! In high school, I was privileged enough to take classes in scientific research, exposing me pretty early on to not just fundamental research skills, but also the joy of exploration and learning. I was a huge physics nerd, hoping to eventually study it in college, but became drawn to environmental science. I wanted to do work that felt like I could address pressing issues in my community and around the world. I attended Boston University, getting my Bachelor's in Earth and Environmental Sciences. I am grateful to the researchers at the Harvard University Herbaria who took a chance on me, offering me a job to conduct research on changing fern phenology using their collections (the job description entailed ferns before I was involved, but it may or may not have been why I was hired).
